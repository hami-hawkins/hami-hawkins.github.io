What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

Websites are made up of many different elements assembled together. There are many ways you can tweak these elements to work for you, and margin, border and padding are just a few. In short, they make up the distance between your content, the edge of the content, and the other elements surrounding your content. This isn’t a great explanation, so let’s think about it a little differently.

Let’s imagine a typical suburban property. Think of the house as your content - this could be text, images, whatever you like! Now let’s put a fence around the property; this is the border. The yard - or area between the house and the fence - is what we in coding call the padding. Finally, you might have a berm or the like outside the fence; this is the margin.
